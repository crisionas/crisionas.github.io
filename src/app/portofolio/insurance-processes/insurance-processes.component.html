Automated Insurance Processes

Characteristics

Project Type: Enterprise

Client: Cegeka, Germany

Period: July 2022 - May 2023

Overview

       The project involved working for a client based in Germany with the goal of optimizing and automating their insurance processes. The objective was to develop a system that would streamline the insurance workflows, offer the ability to initiate processes, generate necessary documents and contracts, enable document signing, provide storage for documents, and send regular notifications to customers regarding changes, reminders, and other relevant information.

       The project involved the development of a system composed of more than 7 microservices and one monolithic web service.

Key Features

  • Process Automation: Developed an automated system to initiate insurance processes, streamline the workflow, and reduce manual intervention.
  • Microservices Architecture: Migrated the monolithic insurance system to microservices for improved scalability, modularity, and maintainability through seamless decomposition and integration.
  • Monolith Maintenance: Ensured the functional, stable, and compatible operation of the existing monolithic system throughout the transition phase until complete decomposition into microservices was achieved.
  • Documents Storage and Generation: Developed a module for generating and storing insurance documents using predefined templates and customer information. Implemented the Repository Pattern to ensure consistent database operations and seamless transaction handling between two contexts: microservices (PostgreSQL) and monolith (MSSQL). This integration ensured data integrity and maintained consistency throughout the system.
  • Notification System: Developed a comprehensive notification mechanism to send daily updates and reminders to customers regarding their insurance processes.
